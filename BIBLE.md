# CURIOUSER — The Keeper's Bible

*A branching playable book of Alice's Adventures in Wonderland, for THE SHELF. Written to be handed
to a fresh session so it can build, not re-decide. Trust this file; verify only where the code
visibly disagrees.*

Working title **CURIOUSER** (from "curiouser and curiouser" — a single evocative word that captures
the deepening-strangeness arc, in the shelf's style). Strong alternates, same game: **WHO ARE YOU**
(the Caterpillar's question — the identity theme, the truest) or **WONDERLAND** (max recognition).
Repo/folder: `alice`. Live target: `https://kylefriesmarketing.github.io/alice/` · a book on THE
ROOM hub.

Companion to `sherlock/BIBLE.md` (ELEMENTARY). Deliberately its opposite: ELEMENTARY is a cold
rational world you *solve*; CURIOUSER is an irrational dream you must *move through without losing
yourself*. Build them as a matched pair and the shelf gains its two most distinctive shapes.

---

## 1. The pitch, and why it is UNIQUE on this shelf

You are Alice. You followed a waistcoated rabbit down a hole and the world stopped making sense. You
are the wrong size for every room, every creature answers a question with a worse one, and the
longer you stay the harder it is to remember your own name. You cannot win Wonderland. You can only
**wake** — and the trick is to wake *as yourself*.

Every other SHELF game runs on cause and effect and a meter that drains toward death. CURIOUSER is
built to be the exception on every axis:

- **No death. New stakes.** You can't die in a dream. You can **sink too deep to wake**, **lose
  who you are**, or **be trapped as another mad fixture of Wonderland**. The horror is dissolution,
  not injury — a register nothing else on the shelf touches.
- **A BIDIRECTIONAL core resource: SIZE.** Alice is constantly the wrong size — too big to fit the
  door, too small to reach the key, drowning in her own tears, filling the Rabbit's house with an
  arm out the window. **Both extremes are failure; the right size is fleeting and situational.** No
  other game has a resource where *more* and *less* are both bad. It is the whole book, made
  mechanical.
- **The world is the antagonist, and it is NONSENSE.** The core verb isn't fight or solve or
  survive — it's *negotiate the impossible*. And the game inverts the normal contract: **being
  reasonable is usually the trap** (§5). That inversion is the design's beating heart and its
  claim to being unlike anything you've shipped.
- **Dream-shape, not journey-shape.** Wonderland loops, contradicts, and repeats its cast. Closer to
  a garden you wander than a road you walk — the anti-NOBODY-voyage.

The genre no SHELF title occupies: **the dream / nonsense game.** That is the reason to build it,
and the reason it reads instantly as "Alice" and not "another branching book."

## 2. Copyright — the trust section (nail this so the build session never panics)

**Alice is public domain, worldwide, twice over. Build freely.**

- **The books:** *Alice's Adventures in Wonderland* (1865) and *Through the Looking-Glass* (1871),
  by Lewis Carroll (Charles Dodgson, died **1898**). Public domain everywhere for over a century —
  life + 70 lapsed in 1968; US pre-1929 is automatic.
- **The art:** **John Tenniel's original engravings** (1865/1871) — the iconic Alice-with-the-
  hairband, the Cheshire Cat, the card gardeners, the Jabberwock — are **public domain** (Tenniel
  died 1914; the images are 1860s–70s). They are our art north star (§10) and free to adapt, trace,
  and reinterpret.
- Characters, imagery, phrases — all free: the White Rabbit, Cheshire Cat, Mad Hatter, March Hare,
  Dormouse, Caterpillar, Duchess, Mock Turtle, Gryphon, Queen/King of Hearts, the whole pack of
  cards; "curiouser and curiouser," "off with her head," "we're all mad here," "drink me / eat me,"
  "six impossible things before breakfast," the Jabberwocky poem.

**The only things to avoid** (separate, live copyrights — do not reference or reskin): **Disney's**
Alice (1951 designs — the specific blue-dress/apron look, character designs, songs), the **Tim
Burton** films, **American McGee's Alice** (the video game), and any specific **modern illustrated
or annotated edition**. As with every SHELF game: adapt the works, **write 100% original prose**,
lean on **Tenniel** for the look. Never paste more than a short, attributed line of Carroll (the
poems are quotable in fragments, but prefer original nonsense in his meter).

## 3. The hard problem, and the core loop

**The problem:** Nonsense resists gameplay. A game needs rules; Wonderland's entire essence is that
rules don't hold or keep changing. Make it a logic-puzzle and you murder the wonder. Make it pure
randomness and you murder agency. Every Alice game dies on this rock.

**The solution — three moves:**

1. **SIZE is the one hard rule that always works.** Amid the chaos, the player gets *one* reliable,
   fully-controllable lever. After the Caterpillar's mushroom (§8) you can grow and shrink at will;
   before it, size lurches from consumables you find. Managing size to fit each impossible room is
   real, learnable agency in a world that otherwise refuses to be learned. **Agency lives in SIZE;
   wonder lives everywhere else.**
2. **Every encounter is a NONSENSE NEGOTIATION with three postures** (§5) — *reason / play-along /
   defy* — and the game **inverts the normal contract: reasoning is usually the trap.** This turns
   "the world doesn't make sense" from a frustration into the actual skill: you *learn the dream's
   grammar* by playing with it, not against it.
3. **The goal is to WAKE, not to WIN.** Progress isn't enemies beaten or a destination reached — it
   is **self kept** and **impossible things accepted** on the way to the surface. This reframes the
   whole game so nonsense is the *medium of victory*, not an obstacle to it.

**The core loop:** ARRIVE (a new impossible room) → **SIZE UP** (are you the wrong size? adjust via
what's on the table) → **NEGOTIATE** (reason / play / defy the creature and its broken logic) →
**DRIFT** (deeper into the dream, or up toward waking) → the next room. Repeat through the garden of
encounters until you wake — well, badly, or never.

**Iron law of the loop:** *the dream never explains itself, and it never rewards the "sensible"
answer by default.* The prose states the strangeness plainly and lets the player decide how to meet
it. Sense is a tool the player brings, and the game is honest that it usually doesn't work.

## 4. The instruments (the HUD)

Where NOBODY has the Name/Wrath/Favor/Loom and ELEMENTARY has the Index, CURIOUSER has a tight,
dream-shaped set — one active resource, one deep stake, one spine, one collectible:

- **SIZE** — the star, and unlike any meter on the shelf: **bidirectional**. A vertical scale from
  *thimble* to *house*, with Alice's true size in the middle. Shown as a little Alice figure
  swelling and shrinking against notches. Too small: slip through mouse-doors, go unnoticed, but
  can't reach, can't be taken seriously, drown in the tears, become prey. Too big: reach, intimidate,
  refuse to be bullied, but fit nowhere, crush the room, terrify the small. **Encounters gate on
  size**; the wrong size at the wrong door is a (non-fatal) branch, not a wall.
- **SELF** — the deep stake (the Caterpillar's "*Who* are *you*?"). Wonderland erodes identity:
  your name slips, your poems come out wrong ("How doth the little crocodile"), the Pigeon calls you
  a serpent and half you believes it. SELF at zero = you don't wake as Alice — you wake hollow, or
  wake as *Mabel*, or don't wake at all. **Defiance (the climax move) requires enough SELF to pull
  off** — a dissolved Alice cannot say "you're nothing but a pack of cards."
- **THE WAKING** — the spine/clock, and a *dream-depth* gauge rather than a countdown. You can sink
  (deeper nonsense, harder to wake, SELF erodes faster) or rise (toward the riverbank). It is drawn
  as water rising or falling over the scene. Certain acts sink you (surrendering to comfort, giving
  up); others surface you (a grounding truth, an act of defiance, a startle). **Dream-logic
  inversion:** the Queen's "off with her head!" is a *startle* — near the surface it JOLTS you awake
  (escape!); sunk too deep, it does nothing and you stay. Panic saves the shallow and fails the deep.
- **IMPOSSIBLE THINGS** — the cross-run collectible (the White Queen: *"sometimes I've believed as
  many as six impossible things before breakfast"*). Each genuinely impossible thing you accept —
  a grin without a cat, a caucus-race everyone wins, a garden that recedes as you approach, time
  that stopped at tea — is catalogued permanently. **Six before breakfast** is a milestone;
  believing enough of them is part of the *good* waking (you can't wake *well* from a dream you
  fought the whole way). This is CURIOUSER's version of NOBODY's Deeds / ELEMENTARY's monographs.

Recurring cast as texture, not meters: the **White Rabbit** (the through-line — always late, always
ahead of you; following him is the closest thing to a map), the **Cheshire Cat** (the one honest
guide — tells you plainly "we're all mad here," recurs and vanishes, the dream's wry conscience),
the **Caterpillar** (grants size-control), the **Duchess/Cook** (chaos and a baby that becomes a
pig), the **Hatter/Hare/Dormouse** (stuck time), the **Queen of Hearts** (the death-that-isn't),
Alice's **sister** on the bank (the voice from the waking world, heard faintly as you surface).

## 5. THE NONSENSE NEGOTIATION — reason / play / defy (the branching heart)

Every encounter offers up to three postures. This is where CURIOUSER branches, and where its
signature inversion lives.

- **REASON** — apply logic, manners, arithmetic, the rules of the waking world ("but a grin without
  a cat is *impossible*," "one *can't* believe impossible things," "it isn't *polite*"). **Usually
  the trap.** Wonderland doesn't run on sense; insisting on it typically fails, offends the creature,
  or sinks you deeper (frustration is a kind of drowning). **BUT** — and this keeps it honest and
  learnable — each encounter hides *one grounding truth* that genuinely cuts through, and finding
  *which* fact is real leverage vs. which is just stubborn is the skill. Reason is a scalpel, not a
  hammer; the game punishes it as a hammer.
- **PLAY ALONG** — accept the impossible on its own terms; answer a riddle with a riddle; run the
  caucus-race; take more tea though you've had none. **Usually the way through**, and how you learn
  the dream's grammar. Builds IMPOSSIBLE THINGS. Rarely triumphant, often correct — the humble
  competence of a good dreamer. Over-used, it sinks you (you can play along so well you forget you
  ever meant to leave — see the *stay* ending).
- **DEFY** — refuse to be governed by the nonsense; stand your ground; grow (literally); "**Who cares
  for you? You're nothing but a pack of cards!**" **The growth move — and the way you finally wake
  from the trial.** Gated by SELF (a hollowed Alice can't summon it) and by timing: defy too early,
  before you've earned the size and the self, and you just get "off with her head" with no jolt to
  wake you. Defy at the right moment and the whole dream comes apart like cards in the wind.

Design rule for authors: in every encounter, make **reason the seductive-but-usually-wrong** option,
**play-along the reliable-but-humble** option, and **defy the powerful-but-conditional** option — and
plant exactly one *reason* path that is the real key, so the player never fully trusts the pattern.
Wrong postures are **branches with authored consequences**, never retries (the SHELF law, shared
with ELEMENTARY).

## 6. Structure — the garden of encounters (a dream, not a road)

Wonderland is a loop, not a line. The book's rough sequence gives the backbone, but the Cheshire Cat
recurs, paths fork and rejoin, and the same creatures reappear wearing the dream's contradictions.
Backbone encounters (each a scene with its own broken logic, in loose order):

1. **DOWN THE RABBIT-HOLE** — the fall (tutorial): the slow dream-fall, the shelves you drift past,
   arriving in the long hall.
2. **THE HALL OF DOORS / THE POOL OF TEARS** — the first size lesson: the tiny golden door, the key
   on the glass table you're too big to reach then too small to grasp; DRINK ME / the cake; weeping
   a pool you then swim in when small. Teaches SIZE as a bidirectional trap in one room.
3. **THE CAUCUS-RACE** — the pool's creatures; a race with no rules where everyone wins and everyone
   gets a prize (Alice's own thimble). Teaches PLAY-ALONG. First IMPOSSIBLE THING.
4. **THE WHITE RABBIT'S HOUSE** — you grow too big and fill it, arm out the window, foot up the
   chimney; the animals throw pebbles that become little cakes. The *too-big* failure made vivid, and
   escaped by eating to shrink.
5. **THE CATERPILLAR** — fully worked in §8. Size-*control* is granted (the mushroom); "*Who* are
   *you*?"; the poem that comes out wrong (SELF). The hinge of the whole game.
6. **THE DUCHESS'S KITCHEN** — pepper, a hurled baby that becomes a pig, the cook and the flung
   crockery, "and the moral of that is—". Chaos; manners fail spectacularly (REASON trap showcase).
7. **THE CHESHIRE CAT** — the honest guide. "We're all mad here." The grin that stays when the cat is
   gone (a keystone IMPOSSIBLE THING). Points you toward Hatter or Queen — a genuine fork.
8. **THE MAD TEA PARTY** — the posture system's purest room: riddles with no answers ("why is a raven
   like a writing-desk?"), time stopped at six o'clock (the stuck clock), aggressive rudeness. No
   size element — pure nonsense negotiation. Over-staying literally traps you here (a *stay* ending).
9. **THE QUEEN'S CROQUET-GROUND** — the roses painted red (false color, a plot-point about lies), a
   croquet of flamingo-mallets and hedgehog-balls that won't hold still, "off with her head" flying
   every minute (the death-that-isn't). The Gryphon and the **Mock Turtle** as a melancholy detour.
10. **THE TRIAL** — the climax: the stolen tarts, the nonsense evidence, Alice **growing back to full
    size** as the dream's grip fails, and the DEFY moment — the pack of cards flies up — and she
    wakes (or doesn't). This is where SIZE, SELF, and THE WAKING all pay off at once.

Non-linearity: the Cheshire Cat can return anywhere; the White Rabbit crosses several scenes; a
player can loop (the garden that recedes as you approach it is a literal recurring room). Structure it
as a small graph with the Cat as the recurring hub, not a strict 1→10.

## 7. Meta / cross-run, and the LOOKING-GLASS expansion

- **The remembered dream.** Like NOBODY's accreting song: on each waking, the dream is half-
  remembered on the title screen, and across runs Alice remembers *more* (or, on bad wakings,
  *less*) — the title art and text deepen with your history. The IMPOSSIBLE THINGS you've ever
  believed persist as a little catalogue ("six impossible things before breakfast" as the headline
  count).
- **The true telling.** The deepest thread (see §9): Wonderland was first told aloud, by a man in a
  boat, to a real child named Alice. The secret ending turns the dreamer into the *teller* — the
  dream that outlives the dreamer by becoming a story. CURIOUSER's "gentle death off the sea" / oar-
  in-the-wheat: the child who kept Wonderland by giving it away.
- **THROUGH THE LOOKING-GLASS — the sequel / NG+.** A gift for a second act, because it is *already
  a game*: Looking-Glass is a **chess problem**. Alice is a white pawn on a giant board; she advances
  square by square (each square an encounter) toward the **8th rank**, where a pawn is crowned
  **queen**. Built-in station structure with a built-in win condition. Its cast is all fresh and all
  PD: **Tweedledum & Tweedledee**, **Humpty Dumpty** ("a word means just what I choose it to mean"),
  the **Red Queen** and her race (*"it takes all the running you can do to keep in the same place"* —
  a perfect anti-progress mechanic), the **White Knight** (the gentle inventor), the **Jabberwock**
  ("'Twas brillig, and the slithy toves…"). Design it as the unlocked second book once Wonderland is
  won: same engine, chess-advancement replaces the dream-garden, mirror-logic replaces nonsense
  (everything backwards — you run to stand still, you remember the future, you must walk *away* from
  where you want to go to arrive). Note in the bible, build after v1.

## 8. Fully-worked template: THE CATERPILLAR (the data schema, proven)

This exists to prove the model is implementable, and it's the right choice because it exercises
**both** pillars at once — SIZE control *and* the SELF/nonsense negotiation. The build session copies
this shape.

**The room.** A three-inch caterpillar on a mushroom, smoking a hookah, regards you with immense
languid contempt. You are currently the *wrong size for everything* (say SIZE = very small, from the
last room). It asks, eventually, around the smoke: *"Who are you?"*

**State on entry:** `size` (probably low), `self`, `waking`, `impossibleThings`. Watch SELF here —
this room can raise or gut it.

**The negotiation (postures):**
- `reason_iknow` (REASON) — *"I— I hardly know, sir, just at present. I knew who I was when I got up
  this morning, but I think I must have changed several times since then."* **Honest, not stubborn —
  the rare REASON that is the real key here:** admitting the confusion *keeps* SELF (you haven't lied
  about yourself), and the Caterpillar, though rude, engages. `self:+1`. → `the_poem`.
- `reason_demand` (REASON, trap) — *"Who are* you*? You're only a caterpillar."* Applying waking-world
  hierarchy. The Caterpillar is unbothered and dismissive; the exchange sours; `self:-1` (you defined
  yourself by contempt for another and lost a little of your own footing), sinks you slightly. →
  `the_poem` (chillier).
- `play_riddle` (PLAY) — answer his impossible questions on their own terms, unbothered by the lack
  of sense. Builds grammar; `impossibleThings +='a caterpillar's counsel'`; the Caterpillar warms by
  one degree. → `the_poem`.
- `defy_leave` (DEFY, req: self ≥ mid) — turn to go: *"I think you ought to tell me who* you *are,
  first."* Standing your ground early. Risky: too soon and he simply calls you back with the promise
  of *"something important"* (the mushroom), which you need — so defiance here is *survivable but
  premature*, and costs a beat. → `the_poem`.

**`the_poem` (the SELF test).** He orders you to recite "You are old, Father William" — and it comes
out of your mouth **wrong**, the words wandering ("How doth the little crocodile…" energy). This is
the identity-erosion beat made interactive:
- `poem_push` — force it, insist the words are right → they get *wronger*; the wrongness frightens
  you; `self:-1`, sink. (Fighting the erosion feeds it.)
- `poem_let` — let the nonsense poem be what it wants to be; laugh at it; **accept that in a dream
  your own memory rewrites itself** → `impossibleThings +='my own poem, rewritten'`; `self:+1` (you
  met the dissolution with humor instead of panic — the whole game's thesis in miniature). 

**The gift (SIZE control).** However the exchange ends, the Caterpillar, uncoiling to leave, gives the
one durable rule of Wonderland: *"One side will make you grow taller, and the other side will make
you grow shorter."* — *"One side of* what*?"* — *"Of the mushroom."* And he's gone.
- From here Alice can **eat from either side** to change size at will — implement as a persistent
  ability `S.flags.mushroom = true` that unlocks a **SIZE control** on the HUD (nibble left = shrink,
  right = grow) available in every subsequent room. **This is the agency pillar switching on.** The
  first use is the comedy of over-correction (chin to feet, the Pigeon/serpent beat) before you
  learn fine control.

**Outcomes / branches (none a retry):**
- High SELF + mushroom → you leave *more yourself than you arrived*, with control over your own size:
  the model outcome, the hinge working as designed.
- Low SELF (pushed the poem, demanded rank) → you leave with the mushroom but *shakier* — the dream
  has a deeper grip; later defiance is harder; SELF-loss endings loom.
- Either way you carry the mushroom forward. There is no failure screen here — only how much of Alice
  walks out.

That is the entire model in one room: a bidirectional SIZE payoff (control granted), a SELF stake
with real up/down branches, the REASON-as-usually-trap-but-once-the-key rule, PLAY building impossible
things, DEFY gated by SELF — and every path authored forward.

**Schema this implies (see §12 for the full version):**
```js
ENCOUNTER = { id, title, backdrop, epigraph, text(str|fn(S)),
  sizeGate?: {min?, max?, onWrong: nodeId},     // wrong-size branch, non-fatal
  consumables?: [ { label, effect:{size} , once? } ],   // DRINK ME / cake / mushroom
  postures: [ POSTURE ], grantsMushroom?: bool }
POSTURE = { id, kind:'reason'|'play'|'defy', t, req?(S),
  key?:bool,               // the one REASON that truly cuts through, per room
  effect:{ self?, waking?, wonder? }, adds?:impossibleThing, go|outcome }
```

## 9. Endings

Per-encounter beats resolve into a run, and the run resolves into a waking (or not). Endings track
SIZE mastery, SELF kept, THE WAKING, and IMPOSSIBLE THINGS believed.

- **★ THE RIVERBANK** *(the true waking)* — you grow back at the trial, throw off the pack of cards,
  and wake on the bank with your sister, the dream sliding off you like water — **as yourself**, and
  keeping the wonder without being kept *by* it. Requires: enough SELF to defy at the trial, enough
  IMPOSSIBLE THINGS to have truly *been* there (you can't wake well from a dream you fought the whole
  way), and SIZE mastered. The book's own ending: the child who can hold both sense and wonder.
- **THE TELLER** *(the secret ending — the deepest note)* — earned across runs (all the impossible
  things believed, SELF kept through a full waking, and the dream remembered rather than lost).
  Wonderland was first told aloud by a man in a boat to a real Alice; here the dreamer becomes the
  *teller* — you carry Wonderland back and give it away, and the dream outlives the dreamer by
  becoming a story that never ends. CURIOUSER's oar-in-the-wheat / Beekeeper equivalent: *the only
  way to keep Wonderland forever is to hand it to someone else.*
- **MABEL** *(SELF to zero, but you wake)* — you surface, but hollow: you've forgotten so much of
  yourself that you wake unsure you're Alice at all. *"I'm sure I'm not Ada… and I'm sure I can't be
  Mabel."* A waking that is its own small grief.
- **THE STAY** *(sank too deep / played along forever)* — you never wake. You take the empty chair at
  the tea-party for good, or become the next thing that grins and vanishes — a new mad fixture of
  Wonderland, content, gone. The dream closes over like the lotus in NOBODY, the give-up in STILL
  BREATHING — but gentle, and smiling, which is worse.
- **THE WRONG SIZE** *(a size-driven bad end)* — trapped by scale: too big to ever leave the Rabbit's
  house / the courtroom, or so small the world loses you in the grass. Non-fatal, but a kind of
  imprisonment in the dream's geometry.
- **OFF WITH HER HEAD** *(the startle)* — the Queen's cry, at the right shallow moment, is not an
  ending but the *jolt that wakes you* (route to a lesser Riverbank); sunk too deep, it's just more
  nonsense and routes you back into the garden. Dream-logic: the same event is escape or nothing,
  depending on depth.

## 10. Art direction — Tenniel's line, dream color

Each SHELF game has one signature look, and CURIOUSER's is chosen to be **the opposite of
ELEMENTARY's fog-and-ink**: **John Tenniel's 1865 wood-engraved line** — the definitive, public-
domain Alice — but flooded with **saturated, shifting, wrong dream-color**. A coloring-book that
colors itself, and colors itself *wrong*: a garden too green, roses being painted red over white,
a sky that changes hue between rooms. Line of the 1860s; color of a fever.

- **Palette:** Tenniel's black engraving line over storybook hues that *drift* — no fixed scheme;
  the dream recolors between scenes. Anchor accents: card-suit red, a poison-bottle green, a
  Cheshire violet-pink, gold-key yellow. When THE WAKING rises, color drains toward the pale, real
  light of the riverbank; when you sink, it super-saturates.
- **Pipeline (identical to NOBODY / ELEMENTARY):** Higgsfield `nano_banana_pro`, prompt as *"John
  Tenniel Victorian wood-engraving line illustration, storybook, hand-tinted with saturated dream
  color, cross-hatched…"*; generate 3×3 scene **sheets**; `upscale_image` to 4k; **slice locally**
  with System.Drawing (the `.work/slice4k.ps1` pattern in `nobody/` — 3.5%/5% inset drops gutters).
  Procedural **SVG fallbacks** in `art.js` in the same engraved-line-plus-flat-dream-color language.
- **Scene keys** ≈ one per backbone room: `fall`, `hall`, `pool`, `caucus`, `rabbithouse`,
  `caterpillar`, `kitchen`, `cheshire`, `teaparty`, `croquet`, `mockturtle`, `trial`, `riverbank`
  (the waking), plus `title` (Alice small before the tiny door, or the Cheshire grin alone in
  foliage) and `og.jpg`.
- **Signature UI art:** the **SIZE gauge** — a little Tenniel-Alice that visibly swells and shrinks
  against notched wallpaper, the screenshot that sells the game; and the WAKING as a waterline that
  literally rises up the scene. Both pure CSS/SVG so they animate.

## 11. Audio direction — a music-box that curdles

Generative WebAudio, no files (identical philosophy to NOBODY's `audio.js` — a `setScene(key,…)`
layering drone + noise-bed + a motif scheduler).

- **The music-box theme** — a simple, sweet nursery/lullaby figure (celesta/music-box timbre) that
  goes **subtly wrong as you sink**: detuning, tempo-warping, notes arriving late or doubled. In a
  shallow dream it's charming; deep, it's a lullaby melting. It **rights itself** only as you
  surface toward waking.
- **The Tea Party** — a clock stuck at six: a tick that never advances, a chime that starts and
  never completes. Time-that-stopped, audibly.
- **The Cheshire Cat** — a grin motif: a rising two-note figure that appears and *vanishes mid-phrase*,
  like the cat — sometimes just the tail of it, floating with no beginning.
- **The Queen** — martial, percussive, croquet-mallet thwacks and a heart-drum; "off with her head"
  as a cymbal-crash startle that (near the surface) bleeds into the waking sound.
- **Waking (the riverbank)** — the real world leaking in: birdsong, water, a sister's faraway voice,
  the music-box finally resolving to a clean, warm cadence. The one fully-consonant sound in the game.

## 12. Stack & architecture — mirror THE SHELF exactly

Copy NOBODY's/ELEMENTARY's lean stack so the build session has zero friction. Vanilla JS, zero deps,
all content data-driven.

```
alice/
  index.html            all DOM + one <style> link; screens: title / game / waking / gallery
  css/style.css         Tenniel-line + dream-color theme; the SIZE gauge + WAKING waterline
  js/data.js            ENCOUNTERS (the garden as data) — split data2.js for Looking-Glass later
  js/engine.js          the loop: scene render, size control, posture negotiation, drift, waking, persistence
  js/art.js             Tenniel-style procedural SVG fallbacks + ART.paint(scene) + ART.sizeGauge(S) + ART.waterline(S)
  js/audio.js           generative WebAudio (curdling music-box, stuck clock, grin motif)
  js/images.js          generated-stills manifest (sliced sheet scenes); SVG fallback when absent
  serve.ps1             copy from repo root (PowerShell HttpListener static server; see nobody/serve.ps1)
  .nojekyll             CRITICAL for GitHub Pages
  README.md  BIBLE.md
```

**Run state `S`** = `{ node, size, self, waking, wonder, mushroom, impossibleThings:[], seen:{}, flags }`.

## 13. Testing recipes (a dream graph soaks differently than a voyage)

- **Waking-reachability (the key test):** from the start node, a graph search must confirm at least
  one path reaches **each** waking under *some* SIZE/SELF/waking trajectory, and that **no encounter
  soft-locks** (every scene, at every reachable size, offers ≥1 forward action — including its
  `sizeGate.onWrong` branch). Write `window.__alSoak(runs)` that random-walks postures/size like the
  NOBODY soak and asserts: 0 dead nodes, 0 stuck states, every `outcome`/`go` target exists.
- **Size-gate integrity:** every `sizeGate` must have a real `onWrong` node (non-fatal branch), and
  every room must be *completable at some achievable size* (you can't gate a room behind a size the
  player can't reach there — check the mushroom/consumable availability upstream).
- **Posture inversion audit:** confirm each encounter has its three postures with the intended
  valence (REASON usually costs/sinks, exactly one `key:true` REASON per room, PLAY builds
  impossibleThings, DEFY gated by SELF) — a lint over the data, not just vibes.
- **Prose sweep** (reuse NOBODY's): render every scene/posture/outcome under representative `S`
  states; flag unbalanced `<em>`, `undefined`/`NaN`/`${` leaks. (NOBODY's sweep caught real bugs.)
- **Verify in-browser via DOM, not screenshots** — the animated title/gauge/waterline pages **time
  out** on the screenshot tool here (grain + SVG loops never idle). Use `get_page_text` /
